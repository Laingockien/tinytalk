# Product Vision

## Vision

TinyTalk helps one parent build a daily English-speaking habit with their 2-year-old child through short, natural conversations.

TinyTalk is not an English course.

It is a conversation companion that provides realistic daily situations, allowing the parent to practice speaking naturally with their child. Every lesson is designed to be completed in just a few minutes and can easily fit into everyday routines such as waking up, eating, bathing, playing, or going to bed.

The goal is not perfect grammar or pronunciation.

The goal is to help both parent and child become comfortable using English as a natural communication tool at home.

---

# Mission

Make daily spoken English simple, enjoyable, and sustainable through short conversation practice.

Parents do not need to prepare lessons, study grammar, or memorize vocabulary. They simply open TinyTalk, choose a topic, and start speaking.

---

# Target User

TinyTalk is designed for one specific user.

- Parent of a 2-year-old child.
- Beginner to intermediate English level.
- Wants to create an English-speaking environment at home.
- Has only 5–10 minutes available for each practice session.
- Prefers speaking over studying grammar.
- Uses both mobile phone and desktop.

---

# Product Philosophy

Children acquire language through meaningful interaction, not by memorizing vocabulary or grammar.

TinyTalk recreates everyday situations through carefully designed conversations. Each lesson represents a small real-life scenario that parents can immediately use with their child.

Examples include:

- Saying good morning
- Asking for water
- Eating breakfast
- Playing with toys
- Reading books
- Bath time
- Going to bed

The product focuses on communication first.

Vocabulary, pronunciation, and repetition naturally develop through daily conversations.

---

# Learning Principles

TinyTalk follows five core learning principles.

### 1. Context Before Vocabulary

Children understand language more naturally when words appear inside meaningful situations.

Vocabulary is never presented as isolated word lists.

### 2. Speaking Before Studying

Parents start speaking immediately.

There are no grammar lessons before practice.

### 3. Small Daily Practice

Each lesson lasts only a few minutes.

Consistency is more valuable than long study sessions.

### 4. Confidence Before Perfection

Parents are not expected to speak perfect English.

If the sentence is understandable, the lesson continues.

TinyTalk encourages communication instead of perfect pronunciation.

### 5. Repetition Through Daily Life

Important sentence patterns appear repeatedly across different everyday situations.

This helps both parent and child remember naturally without feeling like they are studying.

---

# Product Scope

TinyTalk intentionally focuses on doing one thing exceptionally well:

Helping one parent have short English conversations with their 2-year-old child every day.

Anything outside this goal is considered out of scope for the MVP.

---

# Product Characteristics

TinyTalk should always feel:

- Simple
- Friendly
- Calm
- Encouraging
- Child-friendly
- Fast
- Easy to use

The interface should never feel overwhelming or distracting.

---

# Success Criteria

TinyTalk is successful if the parent can:

- Open the website within seconds.
- Find a suitable conversation topic quickly.
- Complete one conversation in under 10 minutes.
- Feel more confident speaking English with their child.
- Return the next day and continue building the habit.

---

# Long-term Vision

The MVP is powered by a curated database of conversation topics.

As the content library grows, TinyTalk can expand with more situations, richer conversations, and optional intelligent features.

Regardless of future improvements, the core philosophy will always remain the same:

**Small conversations. Every day. Real communication.**
