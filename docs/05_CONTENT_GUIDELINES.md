# Content Guidelines

## Purpose

This document defines the content standards for every lesson in TinyTalk.

All conversation topics must follow these rules to ensure a consistent learning experience.

---

# Core Principle

Every lesson should feel like a real conversation between a parent and a young child.

The objective is communication, not language testing.

---

# Target Child

- Age: 2 years old
- Beginner English exposure
- Learns through listening and repetition
- Cannot read independently
- Very short attention span

---

# Parent Profile

- Beginner to intermediate English
- Wants to practice spoken English
- May not have perfect pronunciation
- Practices 5–10 minutes per day

---

# Conversation Rules

Every topic represents ONE small real-life situation.

Examples:

- Wake up
- Breakfast
- Brush teeth
- Bath time
- Going outside
- Reading a book
- Playing with toys
- Bedtime

Do not combine multiple situations into one lesson.

---

# Dialogue Rules

Every conversation should:

- Start with the parent.
- Alternate between Parent and TinyTalk.
- End naturally.
- Sound like a real conversation.

Structure:

```
Parent

↓

TinyTalk

↓

Parent

↓

TinyTalk
```

---

# Conversation Length

Recommended:

- 4–8 dialogue turns.

Maximum:

- 10 dialogue turns.

Lessons should finish within approximately 3–5 minutes.

---

# Sentence Length

Keep every sentence short.

Recommended:

- 2–8 words.

Maximum:

- 15 words.

Avoid long explanations.

---

# Vocabulary Rules

Use:

- Everyday words.
- High-frequency vocabulary.
- Concrete nouns.
- Simple verbs.

Avoid:

- Academic vocabulary.
- Idioms.
- Slang.
- Technical terms.
- Rare words.

---

# Grammar Rules

Prefer:

- Present Simple
- Imperatives
- Yes / No questions
- WH questions
- Short answers

Avoid:

- Passive voice
- Complex tenses
- Conditional sentences
- Reported speech
- Long subordinate clauses

---

# Tone of Voice

The conversation should always feel:

- Warm
- Friendly
- Positive
- Encouraging
- Natural

Never sound like a textbook.

---

# Parent Dialogue

Parent sentences should:

- Be easy to pronounce.
- Be useful in daily life.
- Be natural.

Examples:

- Good morning.
- Come here.
- Drink some water.
- Let's play.
- Are you hungry?

---

# TinyTalk Dialogue

TinyTalk acts as the conversation partner.

Responses should be:

- Natural
- Short
- Friendly

Examples:

- Good morning!
- Yes!
- Okay!
- Thank you!
- I love it!

---

# Translation

Every parent sentence may include a Vietnamese translation.

The translation is only for reference.

It is never spoken aloud.

---

# Acceptable Variants

Parent dialogue may include alternative accepted sentences.

Example:

Expected:

```
Do you want some milk?
```

Accepted:

```
Do you want milk?

Would you like some milk?
```

The meaning should remain the same.

---

# Repetition Rules

Important sentence patterns should appear repeatedly across different lessons.

Example:

```
Let's...

Can you...

Do you want...

I like...

Where is...

Look at...
```

Repetition helps both parent and child build confidence naturally.

---

# Lesson Difficulty

Lessons should gradually become more difficult by introducing:

- New situations
- New vocabulary
- Slightly longer sentences

Difficulty should increase slowly.

---

# Topic Naming

Topic titles should be short.

Recommended:

- 2–4 words.

Examples:

- Good Morning
- Drink Water
- My Dog
- Bath Time
- Let's Eat
- Go Outside

Avoid long titles.

---

# Content Quality Checklist

Before adding a new topic, verify:

- One topic = one situation.
- Parent speaks first.
- Conversation sounds natural.
- Sentences are short.
- Vocabulary is familiar.
- Grammar is simple.
- Topic is useful in daily life.
- Child can understand through context.
- Parent can finish within a few minutes.

If all answers are YES, the topic is ready.
